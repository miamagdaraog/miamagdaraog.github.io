Charlie

21, san diego—california

drenched
and
sobbing
I feel the security of solitude
and imagine the people care
in other hands
distance and time
Time and Distance too.
blue-and-silver the photographers
their cameras, in sullen rebellion,
the dogmatic argument of blueprints that said
she had to fly because the figures proved it.
So she had flown, once airborne,
lifted the weight
question
two thousand miles of unbroken ocean
the night
We are with the night
the last light,
the last
flashes—so many to the minute.
I fly out to sea.
gone
the confidence and the trust,
the inherent belief in security

How do you think your frame of reference affects your art?

I think everything about my life—my personal/familial history, my perception/thought process—affects my art. Without my experience and the told experience of those around me, I wouldn’t have become the person I am today. Thus, my art is a reflection of anything/everything I (subconsciously and consciously) deem important.
