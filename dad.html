Juan

49, san diego—california

It is dark and I am
the lights
the lights are rain, and I am dry.
I am sobbing but
I feel the solitude of escape.
I can imagine people
selfishly triumphant, as if I have
the small sorrow
I left the Irish Sea behind me
distance and time are the same.
Time stopped—and Distance too.
It was the moment I lifted
their cameras, the moment I felt
the earth in sullen rebellion,
the persuasion of argument that said
she had yielded to the question
We are unbroken
We are standing
counting the minute pass
gone now
is confidence and trust
