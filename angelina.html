Angelina

20, oakland—california

It is dark already I am over
the lights are wet,
I am above them
in a sobbing world,
I imagine the people
walking under
I left
behind me like so much time used up.
time are the same. Time stopped—and Distance too.
the moment the photographers aimed their cameras,
she had to fly
Now, where are we bound?
—and the question had frightened me.
We are bound for a place thirty-six hundred miles from here—
So there behind me is
and ahead of me is
It is the last light,
Then I fly out to sea.
The fear is gone now—
something else has taken its place

How do you think your frame of reference affects your art?

Everything is art to me (cliche I know), but I find inspiration in anything—cup of coffee, making eye contact with a stranger, interacting with friends, etc. I think once you stop looking for art and realizing it’s actually perpetually all around you is when it rlly starts to flow.
