Mia

21, san diego—california

the wet rain,
and I
roars in solitude,
So long as I have
care behind me
the persuasion of
blueprints
proved once airborne
are bound for unbroken ocean.
So there ahead of me is
the last
minute.
The fear is gone now
because something else has taken its place;

What did you have for breakfast?

A cinnamon raisin bagel with cream cheese and an iced oat chai.

What is your favorite pastime?

Collaging and making oil pastel drawings of my friends and playing music (pastimes plural).

How do you think your frame of reference affects your art?

Growing up, I was lucky enough to be surrounded by creativity. I learned how it could be a part of everyday life, not a mere hobby. My mom always taught me to see the silver lining in things and I think it's very reflective in my art—a manifestation of seeing beauty in the darkest corners of life! Further, I am constantly inspired by the small and supposedly mundane vignettes around us and much of my art acts as a pedestal for these interactions.
